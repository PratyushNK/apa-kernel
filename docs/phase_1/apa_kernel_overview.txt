What you built
A research-grade autonomous policy adaptation kernel for payment orchestration systems. The core research contribution is not the simulator or the metrics — it's the invariant-preservation guarantee during autonomous policy evolution.

The problem you're solving
Modern payment orchestration systems (routing, retry, failover) run on static manually-tuned policies. When conditions change — gateway degrades, traffic spikes, failure rates shift — human engineers must detect and respond. This is slow, reactive, and doesn't scale.
The naive fix is "just learn the policies automatically." But autonomous policy learning in financial systems is dangerous — naive optimisation can violate critical safety properties like retry bounds, idempotency, SLA contracts, circuit breaker state. A learning agent that discovers "more retries = higher approval rate" will amplify load during an outage until the system collapses.
Your research question: Can a system autonomously evolve operational policies while providing formal guarantees that safety invariants are never violated — even during the adaptation process itself?

Your answer
Instead of learning policies directly, the kernel learns policy adaptations constrained by invariant validation. Every proposed mutation is verified — formally, via TLA+ model checking — before deployment. If the proposed θ violates any invariant, it is rejected and a correction is attempted. The system never deploys an unsafe policy by construction.
This is the Knight Capital argument made rigorous. Knight Capital lost $440M in 45 minutes from an unconstrained autonomous policy deployment. Your kernel makes that class of failure architecturally impossible.

What makes it research-worthy
Three properties that current autonomous agent frameworks don't provide together:
1. Formal safety — TLA+ verification of every proposed policy vector before deployment. Not runtime checks, not heuristics — machine-checked proofs over the full state space.
2. Agentic reasoning over RL — The adaptation agent reasons about why metrics are degrading, which invariant is at risk, and what the minimal conservative fix is. A pure RL agent collapses this into a reward scalar and cannot distinguish a transient burst spike from a structural gateway failure. Your agent can.
3. Temporal decoupling — Adaptation runs on slow deliberate cycles (every 500-2000 transactions) with statistical confidence windows. This is not reactive control — it's structural policy evolution. The cadence is justified by queueing theory (M/M/1 window stability) and matches how real SRE teams operate: observe for long enough to be confident, then act conservatively.

The demo in one sentence
A payment gateway fails mid-simulation. The kernel detects the degradation, reasons about cause in two LLM stages, proposes a minimal policy correction, verifies it against six formal invariants via TLA+, deploys it, and confirms recovery — autonomously, in under 30 seconds, with zero invariant violations.

What this is NOT
It's not a routing library. It's not a retry framework. It's not an RL environment. It's a meta-layer — a kernel that sits above any operational policy system and provides autonomous adaptation with formal safety guarantees. Payment orchestration is the instantiation. The framework generalises to any policy-governed system with definable invariants.
